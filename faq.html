FAQs

Are vaccinations needed?
It is strongly recommended that you contact your travel doctor (or relevant local health authority) and make an appointment to evaluate any vaccinations before arriving in Kenya. Depending on the situation and the countries visited before arrival, some vaccines may be necessary. REMEMBER: if you want to travel to Zanzibar passing through Kenya first, the yellow fever vaccine is mandatory.
What is the best time to visit Kenya?
The tourist season starts from July and ends in April. July, August and September are the best times for safaris, in particular for the Masai Mara nature reserve where you can witness the unique spectacle of the wildebeest migration from the Serengeti. October should instead be the period of small rains, but if you only have this month available to visit Kenya, we still advise you to leave because the rains are sporadic and the tropical climate may surprise you after a rain with a beautiful sunny day . From November to March is by far the best period, especially for the ocean with its dolphin migration, but also for safaris. April will instead be the last beautiful period of the season waiting for the heavy rains from May to the end of June/beginning of July.
What should I not forget for my safari?
Protective sun cream, hat, sunglasses, lip balm. Insect repellent (possibly tropical version, should be applied approximately every 6-8 hours). Clothing must be comfortable, bring long changes for the evening and early morning, t-shirt and shorts for the day, but remember to bring several changes because the red earth of the savannah can be annoying. Also remember a bathing suit because in the accommodation there is often a swimming pool and comfortable shoes (possibly old and not white to avoid the risk of staining them). Also important are essential medications, including painkillers for common headaches, medications for dysentery, paracetamol and eye drops for possible dirt in the eyes. The camera is unforgettable and, if you want to be sure of seeing animals even from a distance, the binoculars. REMEMBER: Drones are prohibited in Kenya.
Do I need a visa to enter Kenya?
All citizens over the age of 16 require a visa to enter the country. This must be obtained before arrival by requesting on the official website ecitizen.go.ke . Be careful because there are several sites that offer the visa application service, but with a surcharge. Furthermore, remember that the passport must have at least 6 months of residual validity and the visa must necessarily be printed in colour.

Are safaris also suitable for children?
From 0 to infinity, safaris are suitable for everyone! For the little ones aged 0-4 years we recommend two-day and one-night safaris (no one-day safaris because they are too tiring), for children aged 5 and up we also recommend multi-day safaris. The little guests are the ones who will have the most fun, to the delight of the older ones. Get excited together with your children, imagine seeing them gaping in front of a lion, or letting them play to find hidden animals in the savannah. We at Discovery Kenya guarantee maximum safety in all travels, the vehicles chosen, the high quality standards in the accommodation offered, the certified guides who will be able to promptly resolve any unexpected events, all you have to do is enjoy the savannah and experience the best trip of your life.
